What is the CER Directive?

Understanding the Critical Entities Resilience Directive and its impact on your organization

The Critical Entities Resilience (CER) Directive is a comprehensive EU regulation that establishes measures to ensure the resilience of critical entities across essential sectors. This directive aims to enhance the security and resilience of critical infrastructure that is vital for the functioning of society and the economy.

Organizations identified as critical entities must implement robust risk management measures, report significant incidents, and maintain comprehensive resilience strategies to protect against both physical and cyber threats.

Key CER Compliance Requirements

Essential obligations for critical entities under the directive

Risk Assessment

Conduct comprehensive risk assessments identifying all relevant threats to essential functions, including physical and cyber risks.

  • Threat identification and analysis
  • Vulnerability assessments
  • Impact evaluation
  • Regular review and updates

Incident Reporting

Report significant incidents to competent authorities within specified timeframes, with detailed impact assessments.

  • 24-hour initial notification
  • Detailed follow-up reports
  • Impact assessment documentation
  • Remediation measures taken

Resilience Measures

Implement appropriate technical, security, and organizational measures to ensure resilience of critical operations.

  • Business continuity planning
  • Backup and recovery systems
  • Security controls implementation
  • Regular testing and validation

Supply Chain Security

Assess and manage risks related to suppliers and service providers that could affect critical operations.

  • Supplier risk assessments
  • Contractual security requirements
  • Third-party monitoring
  • Alternative supplier strategies
